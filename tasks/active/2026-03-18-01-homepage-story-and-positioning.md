## Goal
Make the homepage instantly communicate the project's core idea: a deterministic universe of model-generated brackets that the real tournament is eliminating in real time.

## Why
- This is the most important product/message gap.
- The site currently reads more like a dashboard than a public interactive exhibit.
- Better framing will improve everything else: retention, sharing, pressability, and portfolio quality.

## Constraints
- Keep the message concrete, not sci-fi or gimmicky.
- Do not lead with internal implementation details.
- Keep the design intentional and memorable, not generic dashboard UI.
- Mention the Library of Babel inspiration subtly in credits/about, not as the primary brand.

## Acceptance Criteria
- The homepage hero answers what this is, why it is interesting, and what a visitor can do next.
- The above-the-fold area includes a stronger headline, subhead, and one clear call to action.
- The copy and visual design reinforce "deterministic bracket universe" instead of a generic stats dashboard.
- The About page or credits mentions the Babel inspiration in a tasteful secondary way.
- Browser verification covers desktop and mobile.

## Current Status
- Not started.

## Next Steps
- Draft 3-5 headline/subhead variants.
- Decide the primary CTA that future features will support.
- Rework the homepage layout and supporting copy.
